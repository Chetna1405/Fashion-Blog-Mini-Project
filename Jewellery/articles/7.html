Bridal jewellery ideas

Contemporary bridal jewellery for the mehendi

Arora recommends a multi-layered pearl necklace with modern reversible enamelling. Dubbed as the ‘modern meenakar’, jewellery designer Sunita Shekhawat advises against hand accessories like bangles and bracelets for the mehendi. “Instead, opt for arm bands that you can later wear as choker necklaces. Accentuate your look with a matha patti.”






Contemporary bridal jewellery for the sangeet
Comfort is important as is jewellery that has a quality of movement to it considering dance is at the centre of the sangeet. Arora favours briolettes and recommends dangling earrings and a stack of bangles as the look’s highlight. Pratiksha Prashant, creative director of Kishandas & Co suggests a Victorian suite with emeralds and diamonds.






Contemporary bridal jewellery for the wedding

Yash Agarwal, partner and creative director at Birdhichand Ghanshyamdas feels less is more on the wedding, contrary to popular belief. Pearls, Colombian emeralds and uneven, uncut shapes are the next big thing according to him. A pearl hair veil, such as the one worn by Rhea Kapoor for her wedding, is his top pick. “Wear just one statement necklace. Choose different cuts within one design for a modern look,” adds Agarwal. Gupta has been exploring old-world diamond cuts such as kite, shield and Benarasi for an elevated look for the modern bride.
