3 fine jewellery labels that will refresh your idea of bridal heirlooms

Bridal jewellery in India is a repository of memories that hold unquantifiable sentimental value. Far more than mere accessories, these jewels turn into an act of love that take on the moniker of an heirloom, passed down from one generation to the next. Each polki bracelet, tanzanite earring and mismatched pearl choker set aside with the intention that they will be cherished the same way they were decades ago. These ornate pieces are tasked with the responsibility to be timeless and versatile while still retaining an overall air of opulence. Newfound awareness and appreciation for ancient jewellery-making techniques with millennial and Gen-Z customers who want to embrace these classic styles and local craftsmanship with their bridal jewellery also open the door for a slew of homegrown labels to embrace the rich and colourful essence of Victorian-era jewels.




With roots in Hyderabad that date back 150 years, Kishandas and Co have established themselves as a fixture in the city's history. With a roster of clients that once included the Nizams, the jeweller has evolved to fit the changing times while never faltering on quality and a dedication to honouring uniquely Indian jewellery-making techniques. Their latest offering Esmeraldes is dedicated to sharing and re-creating the 'emerald gaze' that Pablo Neruda likened to a drop of gold in his poem, The Emerald.

A heritage jeweller that dates back to the 1950s, Hazoorilal Legacy is defined by its contemporary spin on antique finishes and fine craftsmanship. Their most recent Summer Bridal 2022 collections consists of bijoux crafted with handpicked natural diamonds, emeralds, rubies, and uncut polki diamonds. Through the new collection that boasts dangling diamonds with emeralds, and ornate necklaces of pink sapphire, amongst a myriad of other bespoke jewels, the design team builds on our inclination towards nostalgia and old-school bridal style.
